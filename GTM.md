# Go-To-Market

## Exact Target User

**Primary:** Engineering Manager at a Series A startup (15–60 people), responsible for the dev tools budget. They approved Cursor for the team 6 months ago, then added Copilot because a few devs asked, then Claude Team because the PM wanted it. Now they're paying for all three and nobody has benchmarked whether any of it makes sense.

**Secondary:** Solo technical founder at a pre-seed or bootstrapped company. They're paying for ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro, and Cursor Pro individually — $60/mo — and haven't asked whether $20/mo in one tool could replace the other two.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

1. **Post on Hacker News as "Show HN: I built a free AI spend auditor for startups"** — HN loves tools that save money and require zero signup. Target: 200+ upvotes, 50 signups from one post.

2. **Post results screenshot on X** — "Audited my AI stack. Was paying $340/mo, should be paying $180. Free tool: [link]" — make it a shareable result, not a product pitch.

3. **Drop in r/ExperiencedDevs and r/startups** — "Built a tool to audit your AI tool spend — found $160/mo in savings in my own stack. Free, no login." Genuine value, not spam.

4. **Cold DM 20 eng managers on LinkedIn** — "Hey, I built a free tool that audits AI tool spend for dev teams. Took me 2 min to find $200/mo in savings. Want to try it?" — personal, specific, not a blast.

5. **Comment on relevant HN threads** — When someone asks "is Cursor worth it" or "how do you manage AI tools budget" — answer helpfully and link the tool naturally.

## Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already has relationships with AI tool vendors and resellers. A direct integration or co-marketing with one vendor (e.g., Cursor or Anthropic) as a "check if you're on the right plan" tool would give distribution no independent builder could replicate. Credex can also seed the tool to its existing customer base as a retention and expansion play.
